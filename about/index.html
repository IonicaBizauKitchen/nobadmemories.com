ABOUT RACHEL SIMONE WEIL

Rachel Simone Weil (b. 1984) is an experimental designer whose work investigates how cultural attitudes toward femininity intersect with video game history and nostalgia. Weil blends fact with fiction to create exhibitions, written works, and electronic artifacts that center the history of forgotten and imagined games for girls. Her work has been shown internationally at venues such as the Dallas Museum of Art, the Platform Centre for Digital and Photographic Arts, and the Victoria and Albert Museum. Weil's critical design projects are united by the mantra "No Bad Memories," the slogan of a long-defunct floppy disk maker.

Weil holds an MFA in Design from the University of Texas and works as a technical evangelist at Microsoft. In addition, she leads FEMICOM, the Feminine Computer Museum and helps to run Fantastic Arcade, the Museum of Human Amusement, and Juegos Rancheros.
